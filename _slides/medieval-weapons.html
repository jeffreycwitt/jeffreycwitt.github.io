## The Flail
## Cons * Easy to hit yourself (especially when the chain is longer than the handle) * Only effective at a certain distance * Requires a kind of "wind up" motion (the weapon, in a way, has to be "charged")
## The Two-Handed Flail
## Advantages * Can yield a heavier blow * due to additional momentum of joined piece * Impact is shocked absorbed * due to the joint breaking the shock rather than passing it down the pole to the hands of the user * It was cheap or even free * For peasants, often forced into military activity and unable to purchase swords, the two-handed flail was a cheap and effective weapon. * By adding studs or spikes the agricultural tool could be made even more dangerous.
## The Hussite Wars (1419-1434) and Peasant Revolts * As a peasant weapon, we see particularly famous examples of the two handed flail in peasant rebellions, like the Hussite Revolt in Bohemia.
