The Council of Clermont: The Call of Pope Urban II - November 1095

Question: How does Pope Urban Speech recall or reference the many causes leading up to Crusade?

Military Reform

"He saw that faith of Christianity was being destroyed ot excess by everybody..." (Peters, p. 49, n. 2)

"Now, let those, who until recently existed as plunderers, be soldiers of Christ..." (Peters, p. 53, n. 7)

Church Reform

"Especially establish ecclesiastical affairs, firm in their own right..." (p. 51, n. 9)

"Uphold the Church in its own ranks altogether free from all secular power..." (p. 51, n. 10)

Byzantine Call for Help Against the Turks

"He heard, too, that the interior regions of romania [anatolia], where the Turks ruled over the Christians, had been perniciously subjected in a savage attack..." (Peters, p. 49, n. 3)

Seljuk Turks become a dominant force in Anatolia in 11th Century.

1071 Battle of Manzikert is decisive for Turkish Control of Anatolia

1075 Seljuk Turks occupy Jerusalem (see SHMA, p. 163)
